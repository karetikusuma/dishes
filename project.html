Andhra Pradesh — Pulihora (Tamarind Rice)

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds, 1 tsp urad dal
  • 1 tbsp chana dal, 2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp peanuts, curry leaves, salt, oil

Method

  1. Heat oil, add mustard, urad dal and chana dal until golden.
  2. Add green chilies, curry leaves and peanuts; sauté.
  3. Add tamarind paste and salt; mix briefly then toss with cooked rice.
  4. Serve warm with papad or pickles.

Goa — Goan Fish Curry

Ingredients

  • 500 g firm white fish
  • 1 onion, 2 tomatoes, coconut milk (optional)
  • Red chili, turmeric, tamarind, kokum (optional)

Method

  1. Sauté onions and tomatoes with spices; add tamarind or kokum for sourness.
  2. Add coconut milk if desired; simmer and add fish pieces gently.
  3. Cook until fish is done; serve with steamed rice.

Karnataka — Bisi Bele Bath

Ingredients

  • Rice, toor dal, mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas)
  • Bisi bele bath powder, tamarind, ghee

Method

  1. Cook rice and dal together with vegetables.
  2. Add bisi bele bath powder and tamarind extract; simmer until thick.
  3. Finish with ghee and roasted cashews; serve hot.

Kerala — Avial

Ingredients

  • Mixed vegetables (yam, carrot, raw banana, drumstick), coconut
  • Curd, coconut oil, curry leaves

Method

  1. Cook vegetables until just tender.
  2. Grind coconut with green chilies to a coarse paste; mix with curd and vegetables.
  3. Finish with coconut oil and curry leaves; serve with rice.

Madhya Pradesh — Bhutte ka Kees

Ingredients

  • Grated corn (fresh), milk, ghee
  • Mustard seeds, cumin, green chilies, salt

Method

  1. Sauté mustard and cumin, add grated corn and cook a few minutes.
  2. Add milk and simmer until the mixture thickens; finish with ghee.

Maharashtra — Vada Pav

Ingredients

  • Potatoes, gram flour, pav buns
  • Green chutney, dry garlic chutney, oil for frying

Method

  1. Make spiced mashed potato filling and shape into patties.
  2. Coat with gram flour batter and deep-fry until golden.
  3. Serve between pav with chutneys.

Odisha — Pakhala Bhata (Fermented Rice)

Ingredients

  • Cooked rice, water, yogurt (optional), salt
  • Fried or mashed vegetables as sides

Method

  1. Soak cooked rice in water and allow to rest (ferment) slightly; season with salt.
  2. Serve chilled with fried potatoes, fried fish or vegetables.

Punjab — Sarson ka Saag

Ingredients

  • Mustard greens, spinach, bathua (if available)
  • Maize flour (for binding), ghee, garlic, ginger

Method

  1. Boil and blend greens to a coarse paste.
  2. Cook with garlic, ginger and maize flour; finish with ghee.
  3. Serve with makki di roti and butter.

Rajasthan — Dal Baati Churma

Ingredients

  • Wheat flour for baati, mixed dal (toor, chana, moong) for dal
  • Ghee, jaggery and dry fruits for churma

Method

  1. Prepare dal with spices and tempering.
  2. Make baatis (dough balls), bake or roast and dip in ghee.
  3. Crush roasted baati with jaggery and ghee to make churma; serve together.

Tamil Nadu — Masala Dosa

Ingredients

  • Dosa batter (rice and urad dal), potatoes, mustard, curry leaves
  • Onion, turmeric, green chilies, oil/ghee

Method

  1. Make dosa on a hot tawa; prepare spiced mashed potato filling.
  2. Place filling on dosa, fold and serve with sambar and chutney.

Telangana — Hyderabadi Biryani

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice, marinated meat (chicken/mutton), yogurt
  • Fried onions, biryani masala, saffron, ghee

Method

  1. Par-cook rice and marinate meat with spices and yogurt.
  2. Layer meat and rice, add fried onions and saffron, then dum-cook until done.
  3. Serve with raita and salad.
